Tribute to Mother Teresa

Biography

Mother Teresa, born Anjezë Gonxhe Bojaxhiu on August 26, 1910, was a Roman Catholic nun and missionary. She dedicated her life to helping the poor and vulnerable, particularly in the slums of Kolkata (formerly Calcutta), India.

She founded the Missionaries of Charity, a religious congregation that operates hospices, orphanages, and other charitable institutions worldwide. Mother Teresa's selfless work and compassion earned her international recognition and several awards, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979.

Legacy

Mother Teresa's legacy continues to inspire people around the world. Her commitment to serving those in need and her unwavering compassion serve as a shining example of humanitarianism. She once said, "Not all of us can do great things, but we can do small things with great love."

Today, the Missionaries of Charity continue their work in over 130 countries, providing care for the sick, disabled, and destitute. Mother Teresa's tireless efforts to alleviate suffering and bring hope to the marginalized have left an indelible mark on the world.
